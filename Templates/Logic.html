Computers

Have you ever wondered why computers need to have electricity in order to work? It may seem that electricity is simply magic that somehow powers the mysteries within the black box of your laptop or tablet. However, the relationship between electricity and computers is quite similar to the one between people and blood. You may have heard that computers are made of many smaller parts called circuits. These can be thought of as tiny roads for the electricity. Here it travels along paths until it reaches gates, where, based on how much energy goes through, a different amount will continue along the path. People often say the language of computers is zeros and ones. This is really simply our way of understanding the difference between low and high energy amounts.

Logic

Everything a computer does can be expressed as a series of operations. While these are not the addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division you normally think of, they function in much the same way. Usually, you will take two (or more) numbers and find a result based on the input and operation. With computer circuits it is the same way. However, instead of any numbers, you can only take a 0 or 1, and we have different definitions for what each operation does.

NOT (~)

A NOToperation is unique in that it only takes one input. Just as a negative sign takes a positive number and makes it negative and vice versa, a NOT will make a 1 into a 0 and a 1 into a 0.

AND (&)

An AND operation will tell take your input and tell you if both the first AND the second are true. It does this by indicating a false output with a 0 and a true one with a 1.

OR

An OR functions much as an AND does, in terms of the output indicating true or false. However, instead of telling you whether both the numbers are one's, it will tell you if you have at least one 1. As a result, whether both of them or just one of the given values are 1's, the output will be a 1. If the input is two 0's though, the output will also be a 0.
